🔒 Privacy Policy

Obskura Photo Cleaner Pro X

Last updated: March 2026  ·  Effective immediately

The short version: Obskura processes everything 100% locally on your device. We do not collect, transmit, or store any of your photos, metadata, or personal information. We never have, and never will.

1. What We Collect

Obskura Photo Cleaner Pro X does not collect any personal data. Specifically:

  • No photos or image files are ever uploaded or transmitted
  • No metadata read from your photos is stored or sent anywhere
  • No usage analytics or telemetry are collected
  • No account creation or sign-in is required
  • No advertising identifiers are used

2. How the App Works

All processing happens entirely on your device:

  • EXIF metadata is read and removed locally using on-device libraries
  • Face detection uses an AI model stored on your PC — no cloud processing
  • Cleaned photos are saved to your chosen local folder
  • App settings and licence status are stored in the Windows Registry on your device only

3. Microsoft Store

If you downloaded Obskura from the Microsoft Store, Microsoft may collect certain diagnostic data as part of the Windows operating system. This is governed by Microsoft's own Privacy Policy and is outside our control.

For trial and licence management on the Store build, we use Microsoft's built-in licensing API. No payment data passes through our systems — all purchases are handled entirely by Microsoft.

4. Direct Purchase (Non-Store Build)

If you purchased directly, your activation password and activation status are stored only in the Windows Registry on your own device. We do not maintain a database of customer activations or link your purchase to your device.

5. Third-Party Libraries

Obskura uses the following open-source libraries, all of which run entirely on-device:

  • Emgu CV / OpenCV — image processing (LGPL 3.0 / Apache 2.0)
  • MetadataExtractor — EXIF reading (Apache 2.0)
  • YuNet 2023 face detection model (Apache 2.0)
  • CommunityToolkit.Mvvm (MIT)

None of these libraries transmit data externally.

6. Children's Privacy

Obskura is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect any information from children. The app's face-blur feature is designed to protect privacy, including that of minors in photographs.

7. Changes to This Policy

If we ever change this policy, we will update the date at the top of this page and post a notice in the app. We will never introduce data collection without explicit notice and your consent.
